# Script

You aren’t suffering from insomnia. Your brain is just trying to sleep the way humans did for thousands of years.

Before the Industrial Revolution, humans didn’t sleep in one big 8-hour block. Instead, they practiced "bifid sleep."

At dusk, people slept for about four hours, then woke up at midnight. This wasn't a mistake—it was "the first wakefulness." For two hours, families read, talked to neighbors, or simply stayed warm. Then, they slept for another four hours.

So, who destroyed this pattern?

In the 1800s, factory clocks took over. Industrialists needed workers operating machines on strict, continuous shifts. Industrialists designed the 8-hour sleep block to fit factory schedules, not human biology.

So, the next time you wake up at 2 AM, don't panic. You're not broken—you're just human. You are sleeping the way nature intended.

# Research Material

*   **Sources**:
    *   A. Roger Ekirch, *At Day's Close: Night in Times Past* (2005) - Extensive archival research on pre-industrial sleeping habits.
*   **Key Facts**:
    *   Bifid sleep was documented in court papers, literature (e.g., Chaucer's *Canterbury Tales*), and diaries.
    *   During the two-hour "first waking" period, people prayed, read, wrote, visited neighbors, or procreated.
    *   Industrialization, gas street lighting, and standardized shift work caused the two-stage sleep pattern to fade by the early 20th century.
*   **Misconception Map**:
    *   *Folk answer*: Waking up at night is an unnatural medical disease.
    *   *Obvious answer*: Humans have always slept 8 hours straight.
    *   *Correction*: Standardized shifts forced the continuous 8-hour block onto us; our bodies naturally drift back to segmented sleep.
