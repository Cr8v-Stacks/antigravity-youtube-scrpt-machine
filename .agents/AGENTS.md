# Project-Scoped Rules

## Scripting Guidelines & Editorial Style

### 1. Inclusive Hooks & Audience Assumptions
*   **Never assume the viewer is married or engaged**: Do not start scripts with direct personal statements like "look at your left hand" or "are you married?". This alienates single, divorced, or younger viewers.
*   **Use universal framing**: Start scripts by examining the general cultural perception of the object or symbol (e.g., "We all know the grand romantic gesture: the velvet box, the bended knee, and the shiny band of metal that seals the deal").

### 2. Conversational & Story-Driven Tone (Anti-Robotic)
*   **Read like a story, not an article**: Avoid structured, formulaic essay phrasing, robotic lists, or clinical language. The narration should sound like a dryly witty, warm story told by a friend.
*   **Inject light humor and romanticism**: Balance historical cynicism with subtle romanticism and humor. Make the script feel human, warm, and engaging. Keep the viewer hooked through narrative progression and irony, not listicle bullet points.

### 3. Visual Pacing & Multi-Image Segments (High-Retention)
*   **Avoid stretching single images**: Do not show a single static image for more than 2-3 seconds of narration.
*   **Split voiceover blocks into multiple visual beats**: Even if a sentence is spoken continuously (e.g., 5 seconds), split the timeline into multiple distinct shots with unique image prompts matching the specific nouns or actions as they are spoken.
*   **Visual-to-Audio Mapping**: Align image cuts to specific words (e.g., cut from "lying awake" to "tossing and turning" to "warm milk" inside the same 5-second voiceover sentence).
